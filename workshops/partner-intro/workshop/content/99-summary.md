## What You Covered

**Module 1 — Container Fundamentals**

Containers are the packaging unit of cloud-native applications. Like a VM but stripped to
just the application and its dependencies — megabytes instead of gigabytes, seconds instead
of minutes, hundreds per host instead of tens. Every workload on NKP is a container.

**Module 2 — NKP Kommander Platform Tour**

Kommander solves the enterprise problems that Kubernetes leaves open: multi-cluster management,
federated RBAC, policy enforcement, and a platform add-on catalog. Configure access once in a
workspace; every cluster — present and future — stays in sync automatically.

**Module 3 — Ecommerce on NKP**

A 4-service microservices application running with full Istio service mesh observability —
zero code changes required. GitOps delivery via ArgoCD means deployments and rollbacks are
a Git branch switch. Kiali and Jaeger surface incidents before anyone opens a log file.

## The Partner Opportunity

Your customers are running VMs on Nutanix today. The path to containers runs through the
same infrastructure — no rip and replace. NKP sits on top of AHV, adds Kubernetes, and wraps
it with the enterprise management layer that makes it usable at scale.

The pitch is not "replace your VMs." The pitch is: **"Add NKP to what you already have, and
your customers get the platform their developers are asking for — without changing infrastructure."**
